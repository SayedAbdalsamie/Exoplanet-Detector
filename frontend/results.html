Understanding the detection results

A transit signal occurs when a planet passes in front of its host star, causing a temporary dip in the star's brightness. These periodic dips in the light curve are the primary method used to detect exoplanets. The depth and duration of the transit can tell us about the planet's size and orbital period.

Our AI model is trained on thousands of confirmed exoplanets from NASA's Kepler and TESS missions. It uses deep learning to identify subtle patterns in light curves that indicate the presence of a planet. The model analyzes features like transit depth, duration, periodicity, and shape to make its prediction.
