About Exoplanet Detector

Discovering new worlds through the power of artificial intelligence

Project Overview

The Exoplanet Detector is an AI-powered web application developed for the NASA Space Apps Challenge 2025. Our mission is to democratize exoplanet discovery by making advanced machine learning tools accessible to researchers, students, and space enthusiasts worldwide.

By analyzing light curves from NASA's space telescopes, our system can identify the subtle brightness variations that indicate a planet passing in front of its host star. This transit method has been responsible for discovering thousands of exoplanets, and our AI model helps accelerate this process.

The application combines cutting-edge deep learning techniques with intuitive data visualization, allowing users to upload their own stellar data or explore sample datasets from real NASA missions.

NASA Datasets

Kepler Mission

NASA's Kepler space telescope discovered thousands of exoplanets by monitoring the brightness of over 150,000 stars.

TESS Mission

The Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) is surveying the entire sky to find planets around nearby stars.

K2 Mission

The K2 mission extended Kepler's exoplanet hunt, discovering hundreds of additional planets in different regions of the sky.
